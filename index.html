    Edmund Burke (1851–1919)

    Edmund Burke was one of Toronto's most prolific architects, responsible for dozens of landmark churches, commercial buildings, and civic structures across Ontario. Born in Toronto on 31 October 1851, he apprenticed with his uncle Henry Langley and formed the partnership Langley & Burke in 1873.

    Over a career spanning nearly five decades Burke worked under several firm names: Langley & Burke (1873–1892), Burke & Horwood, and Burke, Horwood & White. He designed in the Gothic Revival, Romanesque Revival, and Chicago School styles, and served as President of the Ontario Association of Architects.

    His most celebrated works include the Prince Edward (Bloor Street) Viaduct (1918), the Robert Simpson Department Store (1896–1923), and many of Toronto's surviving Victorian churches. He died on 2 January 1919.
